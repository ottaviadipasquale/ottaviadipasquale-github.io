Hi, I’m
Ottavia Dipasquale

Post-doctoral research associate at King's College London

The key goal of my research is to develop neuroimaging biomarkers for pharmacological and clinical studies using novel multimodal techniques that combine the molecular information of the neurochemical mechanisms of the brain with its functional activity at rest, measured with functional MRI.
These biomarkers are particularly useful:

  • In drug challenges, to explore the effects of pharmacological manipulation of brain networks from a molecular perspective.
  • In clinical studies, to provide insights into the neurochemical mechanisms underlying functional alterations that would guide treatment development, choice and prediction of response.

Research interests and activity

STUDYING BRAIN FUNCTION AND CONNECTIVITY

Sequence development and testing, acquisition and analysis of resting state BOLD fMRI, ASL and CMRO2 data.

PHARMACOLOGICAL STUDIES

Development of REACT, a multimodal method that uses PET atlases to enrich resting state fMRI analysis. Application in healthy populations and clinical cohorts to tackle different limitations of drug fMRI studies.

TEACHING

I give lectures in the MSc Neuroscience programme on “Analysis of Resting state fMRI data”. I also organise workshops on resting state fMRI analysis for PhD students and post-docs.

STUDENT SUPERVISION

I'm involved in postgraduate supervision.
Please get in touch if you have a background in engineering or neuroscience and would like to discuss possible MSc and PhD projects.
